That Time I Got an Old Chinese Lady To Spike My Friend's Drink

During my study abroad in Chengdu, China, we visited an "ancient town," a town where cultural customs are preserved as if the region hadn't advanced past the 1950s, half as a tourist attraction, half as a way for the locals to live.

While we were there, the study abroad group split up, and I was with a group of friends that I had met at the program. Many of them were new to Chinese culture, and were fascinated by their surroundings here.

My friend and I wandered into a rice wine shop, where an old lady was selling different kinds of homemade rice wines. After talking to the lady, she said she sent her son to MIT in the United States, and found it novel that I, a Chinese American, was visiting China for study abroad.

After developing a rapport, she offered us to try a sample of the wine. Now, if you haven't heard of Chinese rice wine before, the stuff is about 35-60% alcohol by volume. While I knew what I was getting into, my friend did not.

While I held my sample in my hand, it was like the shop owner and I connected, as we watched my friend take a sip. As he swallowed and the wine burned his throat, as he recoiled, and as turned to me to ask me what the heck he had just drank, both the shopkeeper and I burst out laughing at him. At my friend's expense, I bonded with a stranger and created a moment I will never forget.
